# Glossary of Swiss Geographic Classification Systems

## Administrative units

### Municipalities (Gemeinden)
The smallest administrative unit in Switzerland. Each municipality has a unique
BFS number (Gemeindenummer) assigned by the Swiss Federal Statistical Office.
There are approximately 2,100 municipalities (declining over time due to mergers).
Boundaries change every January 1st.

### Districts (Bezirke)
An intermediate administrative level between municipalities and cantons.
Not all cantons use districts — some larger cantons (e.g. Zurich, Bern) do,
while smaller cantons do not. Identified by BFS district numbers.

### Cantons (Kantone)
Switzerland's 26 federal states. Each has a two-letter abbreviation (e.g. ZH, BE, GE)
and a BFS canton number (1–26).

## Statistical regions

### MedStat Regions
106 regions used in the BFS Medical Statistics of Hospitals
(Medizinische Statistik der Krankenhäuser). Municipalities are aggregated into
MedStat regions for reporting inpatient hospital statistics.
Identified by a 4-character code (e.g. `ZH01`).

### MS-Regions (Mobilité Spatiale / Raumplanungsregionen)
110 regions defined for spatial mobility and planning analysis.
A mid-level geography between districts and cantons.

### Labour Market Regions (Arbeitsmarktregionen)
Functional regions defined by commuting patterns, used in labour market analysis.

### Agglomerations (Agglomerationen)
Urban agglomerations defined by BFS, grouping a core city with surrounding
municipalities that have strong functional ties to it.

### NUTS Regions
EU Nomenclature of Territorial Units for Statistics, applied to Switzerland
for cross-national comparisons:
- **NUTS-1**: Switzerland as a whole
- **NUTS-2**: 7 large regions (Grossregionen)
- **NUTS-3**: 26 cantons

## Other identifiers

### PLZ (Postleitzahl / ZIP codes)
Postal codes assigned by Swiss Post. Note: PLZ areas do not align with
municipality boundaries — a municipality may have multiple PLZs, and a PLZ
may span multiple municipalities.

### Locality codes (Ortschaftsverzeichnis)
Swiss Post maintains a locality register with codes for named places within
municipalities, useful for address-level work.
